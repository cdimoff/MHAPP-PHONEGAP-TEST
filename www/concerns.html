Concerns

How to Act on Concerns

  • When addressing performance issues, be honest, up-front, and caring
  • Be clear about concerns and expectations related to performance, workplace, or behavior
  • Be specific about your concerns
    • Instead of saying "your sales performance needs improvement", say "Over the past few weeks your sales figures have fallen by 15%. This isn't like you and I'm concerned"
  • Use open questions
    • Examples: "I'm sure you know that you're an important part of our team and that your contributions to X, Y, and Z, have meant a lot to me. I have noticed that over the past few weeks you have missed a number of deadlines and I'm concerned because this isn't like you. What's going on? What can we do to help?"
